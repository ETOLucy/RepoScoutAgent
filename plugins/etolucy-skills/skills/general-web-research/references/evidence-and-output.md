# Evidence and Output Reference

## Source ledger

Use a table with these fields:

| ID | Claim supported | Source and URL | Published/updated | Accessed | Tier | Evidence note | Caveat |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Use one row per source-claim relationship when a source supports unrelated claims.

## Research plan

For a substantial investigation, write down:

- decision to support;
- geographic and time scope;
- inclusion and exclusion rules;
- comparison criteria;
- query families and languages;
- likely primary sources;
- stop condition.

A practical stop condition is reached when every decisive criterion has at least one strong source, repeated searches yield no new material candidates, and remaining unknowns require private or direct information.

## Claim language

- **Verified fact:** Directly supported by an appropriate primary source.
- **Reported claim:** Attributed to the person or publisher making it.
- **Inference:** A reasoned conclusion whose premises are cited.
- **Unknown:** Evidence is missing, inaccessible, stale, or contradictory.

Prefer calibrated language:

- "The department lists..." rather than "The department has..." when inventory may be incomplete.
- "Two public posts alleged..." rather than "Students say..." when the sample is tiny.
- "No public evidence was found" rather than "There is none."

## Decision matrix

Score only when it improves clarity:

| Criterion | Weight | Candidate evidence | Score | Confidence |
|---|---:|---|---:|---|

Use a consistent scale. Explain anchors, for example:

- 5: direct, current evidence strongly meets the criterion;
- 3: partial or indirect evidence;
- 1: evidence indicates a poor fit;
- N/A: insufficient evidence.

Do not silently turn N/A into zero. Provide an unweighted evidence table when confidence is too low for ranking.

## Recommended report skeleton

```markdown
# Research title

> Researched on YYYY-MM-DD. Scope and freshness note.

## Bottom line

## What matters for the decision

## Findings

## Candidate comparison

## Risks and unknowns

## Recommended actions

## Sources
```

For claims that depend on non-public operating practices, add a fair-process note: public information may be incomplete; verify the decisive conditions directly with multiple relevant stakeholders.
